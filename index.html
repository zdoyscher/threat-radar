The share dip below 50% cuts toward owners: clients now have several near-free substitutes, not one — which makes the "why am I paying a human?" question louder, not quieter.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

Fee compression at the root, hitting all three segments at once. The battleground is the mid-market — startups, agencies, and independent operators willing to route work to whatever is cheapest and good enough — and "cheapest" now includes "free and frontier-class." The 2026 field reports are blunt: one agency owner says a client demanded an 80% fee cut because "they could get better results from ChatGPT," another cut their team 60% to stay profitable, and early-career sales/marketing roles have shed roughly 20% of headcount. Brookings' Molly Kinder calls it the "messy middle": most jobs survive, but losses concentrate in exactly the best-paid cognitive office work advisory firms sell. A quiet OpenAI weekend changes none of it; the cheaper-and-freer-tokens trend — and now a government-oversight regime forming around it — is doing the work right now.

This threat reaches you indirectly, which is exactly why owners under-rate it. Every content platform, chatbot, research assistant, ad-creative generator and "AI strategist" product in your client's stack buys intelligence wholesale and resells it. When wholesale collapses toward zero and can never rise again, three things follow. First, the cheap tier gets good. The $20–100/mo tools that already replace a content retainer stop being a downgrade and start being a defensible choice. Second, your competitors' costs fall to yours. Any advantage you built on "I have the good model and my competitor doesn't" evaporates — everyone has it. Third, and most uncomfortable: the price of output becomes public knowledge. Your client can look up what a million tokens costs. When the raw material of a deliverable is measurably worth cents, an invoice that prices the deliverable itself becomes indefensible — not because your work is worthless, but because you priced the wrong thing. The firms that survive this will be selling the decision, the accountability, and the relationship. The ones that don't will be selling documents in a market where documents are free.

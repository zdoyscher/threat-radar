01
CHATGPT · GPT-5.5 / 5.5 INSTANT · OPENAI

The chatbot on every client's desktop does the advisory work — and is becoming a superapp.

THE SHIFT → For ~$20/month, ChatGPT produces the strategy, plans, copy, and analysis clients used to pay an advisor to think through — and OpenAI is now rebuilding it into one app that also acts.

CRITICAL TRAJECTORY · worsening fast VELOCITY · SPIKING LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

"Chat is dead." A senior OpenAI employee said exactly that to the Financial Times (June 7), and the whole AI internet ran with it overnight. OpenAI is merging the ChatGPT chatbot, the Codex coding platform, and the Atlas browser into a single agent-driven superapp — and leaked developer docs point to a general-availability flip as soon as June 9 (tomorrow). Stack on the still-open GPT-5.6 rumor (prediction markets now ~89% for a release by June 30) and a fresh memory upgrade (2× capacity for Plus/Pro), and every "ChatGPT replaced my ___" video is climbing again. This pins #1 and keeps it spiking hardest on the board.

CONTEXT — WHY IT'S #1

This isn't a niche tool — it's the assistant clients already have open, running on GPT-5.5 Instant (which OpenAI says cut hallucinated claims sharply vs the prior default on high-stakes prompts). ChatGPT handles on the order of 2 billion+ queries a day for a user base near 1 billion weekly and is now a top-5 website worldwide. An owner can paste their situation in and get a competent campaign plan, competitor breakdown, coaching reframe, or first-draft strategy in seconds — and now, increasingly, have it execute the next step too. It doesn't have to beat you — it just has to be good enough that the client questions the gap between your invoice and a subscription.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

Fee compression at the root, hitting all three segments at once. One agency owner described a client demanding an 80% fee cut because "ChatGPT gets better results," then walking when they couldn't match it. Fresh 2026 industry surveys say roughly 1 in 4 agencies have already had clients ask for lower prices because AI makes the work look cheaper, and execution-layer tasks (content, scheduling, email, reporting, keyword research) are the first to get insourced. The superapp move accelerates this: when the tool that gives the advice can also do the task, the "but who implements it?" defense narrows.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
ChatGPT (GPT-5.5 / 5.5 Instant · superapp) Claude (Opus 4.8) Google Gemini (3.5 Flash) Microsoft Copilot

MOST EXPOSED: ALL — agencies · consultants · coaches

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Sell the outcome, not the answer.
  1. In every sales conversation, say the line out loud: an AI gives you an answer; I deliver the result, the implementation, and the accountability for it. Make the client describe what happens after the answer — that gap is your offer.
  2. Stop shipping the strategy doc as the deliverable. Repackage and reprice around done-with-you / done-for-you execution and an outcome a prompt can't produce.
  3. Use GPT-5.5 / Claude yourself to cut delivery cost and lift margin — but never resell the raw output.
REALITY CHECKThis is a repositioning of what you sell, not a messaging tweak — and the superapp shift makes it more urgent, not less. If your core offer is "I'll tell you what to do," that offer is the thing under threat — plan to rebuild it around implementation, accountability, or a specific result.
02
CHATGPT AGENT · CODEX · GEMINI SPARK · MANUS

Agents that operate your apps just became the whole strategy.

THE SHIFT → Not just answering — these systems take multi-step actions across apps, files, inboxes, and the web, doing execution work end-to-end with a human only approving. OpenAI now says this is the product.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · emerging — climbing fast VELOCITY · SPIKING LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS · HOLDS #2 · VELOCITY SURGE → STORY OF THE DAY)

The agentic wave isn't just loud this morning — it's the headline. OpenAI's "chat is dead, agents are the future" pivot (FT, June 7) reframes its entire flagship around autonomous action, with the superapp folding Codex (now with Computer Use on Windows) and the Atlas agentic browser into one app. Layer on Gemini Spark rolling to AI Ultra subscribers, Manus (independent after Meta's takeover was blocked), and Claude Opus 4.8's agentic gains, and "the year agents went mainstream" is the dominant AI story on every platform today. It still sits at #2, not #1, on one honest point: on structural severity agents are emerging, not finished — today's agents still run mostly human-in-the-loop. This is a velocity surge at a held rank, not a severity upgrade.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

OpenAI folded Operator + Deep Research into ChatGPT Agent, gave Codex Computer Use on Windows (it can now see, click, and type in Windows apps), and runs Atlas Agent Mode end-to-end inside the browser — all converging into the new superapp. Gemini Spark executes Gmail/Docs/Sheets/Slides tasks in the background, even with your phone locked. Claude Opus 4.8 (1M context) runs strong local file and document work. Manus runs long, multi-step autonomous jobs in its own sandbox — and is still independent: regulators blocked Meta's reported $2B takeover. Alongside them, build-it-yourself tools like Lovable, v0, and Bolt let non-developers ship working sites and apps from a prompt.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

This is the frontier that, as it matures, automates whole processes rather than single tasks — the operational and build work many agencies and consultants resell. The honest reason it isn't #1 on severity: today's agents still complete only a fraction of open-ended desktop tasks reliably and mostly run human-in-the-loop. Loud attention, real trajectory — not yet a full replacement. But when the biggest AI company on earth declares chat dead and bets the house on agents, "try an agent on your actual work" stops being a niche experiment overnight.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
ChatGPT Agent + Codex (Windows) Gemini Spark Manus (independent) Claude Computer Use (Opus 4.8) Lovable / v0 / Bolt (build)

MOST EXPOSED: ops/automation & web-dev agencies · process-heavy consultants

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Get in front of the wave — don't wait to be hit by it.
  1. Start using ChatGPT Agent / Codex / Gemini Spark / Claude Computer Use / Manus on your own operations now. Build the cost-and-speed advantage before your clients or competitors do — the superapp is about to put these in front of everyone.
  2. Productize agent-run workflows ("we run X end-to-end") so the automation is your leverage and margin, not your replacement.
  3. Put it on a weekly watch. Its trajectory is the steepest on this board — re-check capability each pass (OpenAI went from "agents are a feature" to "agents are the product" in a single news cycle).
REALITY CHECKThis isn't a fire to put out today — agents still run mostly human-in-the-loop and miss a chunk of open-ended tasks. The real risk is complacency: early adopters become the low-cost, high-speed option that undercuts everyone who waited, and the superapp shortens the runway to "everyone has tried this."
03
GOOGLE AI MODE + AI OVERVIEWS · GEMINI 3.5 FLASH

Search answers the question — so the click never reaches you.

THE SHIFT → Google now resolves the query on the results page. The blog-and-SEO funnel that quietly fed advisory businesses their leads is collapsing at the top.

CRITICAL TRAJECTORY · structural, irreversible VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT MOVED (THIS PASS · HOLDS #3)

Nothing got smaller here — Google just had a quiet news day while OpenAI ate the cycle. Gemini 3.5 Pro is in internal testing (public expected this month) and Gemini Omni/Deep Think are still rolling, but there was no acute spike today. Structurally it remains one of the two biggest threats on the board — a CRITICAL that a loud OpenAI day must never bury. It holds #3 above the HIGH-severity threats precisely because the blend protects structural giants from quiet news days.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

AI Mode (default on Gemini 3.5 Flash) has passed 1 billion monthly users with queries growing fast, and AI Overviews now appear on roughly 48% of all queries (up from ~34.5% in December 2025). Around 60% of Google searches now end without a click; when an AI Overview shows, the zero-click rate is about 83%, and inside AI Mode it's roughly 93%. "How-to" and informational queries — exactly what advisors publish to get found — are hit hardest.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

If your pipeline depends on ranking and capturing the click, that pipeline is shrinking fast. Fresh 2026 data shows roughly 73% of B2B sites lost meaningful traffic across 2024–25 (average YoY decline ~34%), and big publishers like HubSpot (~70–80% organic loss) and Business Insider (~55%) have reported steep declines amid AI search and algorithm shifts. Your content increasingly feeds the AI's answer without ever sending the reader to you — but note the flip side: brands cited inside AI answers earn about 35% more organic clicks, and AI-search visitors convert at a 2×–27× premium. Citation is the new ranking.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Google AI Mode + AI Overviews Perplexity ChatGPT Search

MOST EXPOSED: SEO/content agencies · content-led consultants & coaches · publishers

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Become the cited source — and stop renting traffic.
  1. Run the visibility audit (10 min): search your category in Google AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Perplexity. Note where you're absent. This is also your fix-list for Threat 04.
  2. Restructure cornerstone content for AEO/GEO: lead with the direct answer, clear headings, specific numbers, and schema. AI cites concise, authoritative, well-structured sources — not 2,000-word warm-ups.
  3. Build channels AI can't intercept: an owned email list, referral/partner loops, a community, and presence where humans still go (YouTube, LinkedIn, podcasts).
REALITY CHECKYou cannot out-SEO the zero-click era. The structural move is reducing dependence on organic clicks and rebuilding pipeline across owned + relationship channels. That's real work over months, not a plugin.
04
PERPLEXITY · + COMET BROWSER

The answer engine is the buyer's new gatekeeper.

THE SHIFT → Prospects research — and shortlist — vendors inside an AI. If it doesn't name you, you're not in the running, however good you are.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · rising — climbed to #4 VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT CLIMBED THIS PASS (▲ +1 → #4)

The agentic-browser war reignited overnight. OpenAI folding its Atlas browser into the superapp put "AI browser that shops and acts for you" back at the center of the conversation — and Comet is the direct rival in that fight. Comet is free worldwide, now on iOS/Android/Mac/Windows (the iOS app just got an 8-feature upgrade), integrated into Samsung Internet, and Perplexity just launched Comet Plus ($5/mo, pays publishers). Enough fresh momentum to edge past ad-automation to #4 on the blend; structurally it's unchanged.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Perplexity (~$22B valuation, 100M+ monthly users, used inside 92% of the Fortune 500) is the purest example: its Comet browser — free worldwide since March 2026 — will compare options across retailers and add the pick to your basket. The buying behavior is mainstream: recent 2026 buyer surveys find roughly 51% of B2B software buyers now begin research in an AI chatbot, and 85% of buyers stick to a "day-one" vendor list shaped before they ever talk to you. Being un-cited by the AI is the new page-10-of-Google — and with Atlas vs Comet now a public arms race, the AI is doing more of the shortlisting.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

The moment of competitive selection moves out of your control. A growing share of prospects arrive pre-filtered by a model that already decided who's "worth contacting." You can be excellent and never get the call — because the shortlist was written by an AI that never heard of you.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Perplexity + Comet ChatGPT + Atlas (B2B research default) Google Gemini

MOST EXPOSED: any advisory firm that wins clients through inbound & discovery

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Engineer the consensus that makes the AI recommend you.
  1. Manufacture consensus signals. AI recommends what it sees agreed across independent sources. Get listed and reviewed on your niche's directories and review sites (G2-type), earn third-party mentions, and show up genuinely in the Reddit threads, YouTube videos, and forums where your buyers ask questions.
  2. Publish citable proof: named case studies with real numbers. Vague "we're great" copy is invisible to a model; a specific, verifiable result is quotable.
  3. Niche down hard. AI surfaces the specialist ("best X for Y"), not the generalist. Tighten positioning to a category you can credibly own.
REALITY CHECKThis compounds slowly — citations build over weeks to months and can't be bought overnight. Start now; once you're the consensus pick, it's a moat competitors can't quickly copy.
05
META ADVANTAGE+ / ANDROMEDA · + OPENAI ADS · GOOGLE PERFORMANCE MAX

Autonomous ad systems run the media buying you bill to manage.

THE SHIFT → The ad platforms now automate audiences, placements, budgets, and creative selection — the work paid-media agencies charge a retainer to do.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · accelerating — slipped to #5 VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT MOVED (THIS PASS · ▼ –1 → #5)

Still genuinely accelerating — the Andromeda rollout ("creative is the new audience") keeps reshaping paid-social playbooks, and OpenAI's self-serve Ads Manager (CPC bidding added, the $50k minimum removed) keeps the "do we even need an agency to run this?" question alive among SMBs. But there was no fresh acute spike here today, so the agentic-browser surge edged Perplexity past it. Structural threat unchanged; pure velocity reshuffle.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Meta Andromeda — a neural retrieval engine — scans millions of ads in real time and shortlists the best before the auction even begins; Advantage+ hands audiences, placements, and budgets to the algorithm, and advertisers turning on its AI targeting have seen around 22% higher ROAS than manual setups. On the other side, Google Performance Max + AI bidding decide placements, bids, and formats automatically, and OpenAI's Ads Manager opens a third self-serve channel (CPC + CPM, no minimum spend) right inside ChatGPT — with Dentsu, Omnicom, Publicis and WPP already on board as partners. Agentic ad tools now let an assistant draft and launch a campaign from a single prompt, cutting setup from ~45 minutes of clicks to one instruction.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the platform optimizes better than a human dashboard-jockey, "we manage your ads" stops justifying a retainer. The defensible work narrows to creative, offer, measurement, and strategy — a much smaller surface than the full-service media-buying fee most agencies still quote.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Meta Advantage+ / Andromeda Google Performance Max OpenAI Ads Manager

MOST EXPOSED: paid-media / performance agencies and freelancers

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Move up the stack the algorithm can't reach.
  1. Stop selling "campaign management" as the core deliverable. Reposition and reprice around the three layers automation can't do: creative volume & testing, offer/funnel strategy, and first-party-data + measurement (connecting CRM/site data is shown to cut CAC meaningfully — and it's provable value).
  2. Become the creative engine. Advantage+ rewards many creative variants; sell creative testing at scale as your product.
  3. Tie the fee to growth/outcomes, not hours spent in a dashboard.
REALITY CHECKThe flat "% of ad spend to run the account" model is structurally shrinking as the platforms automate the buying. This needs an offer migration to creative + strategy + data — not a louder pitch for the old service.
06
OCOYA · JASPER · PREDIS.AI

All-in-one content agents produce the work you bill for.

THE SHIFT → Dedicated platforms now generate, design, schedule, and report on content end-to-end — the execution layer that fills agency timesheets.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · steady erosion VELOCITY · STEADY LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

A mature, crowded category — consistent buyer interest but no acute spike in view velocity this pass. Real structural pressure, low headline volatility. Watch item: Jasper Agents orchestrating multi-channel campaigns blurs this card into the agents cluster at #2 — and with the superapp making "an agent that runs your content" the headline framing, expect this one to heat up if that crossover goes viral.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

These tools market straight at you: Ocoya's pitch is literally "You don't need to hire a social media agency anymore" (it now serves 600,000+ customers), auto-turning a product listing or price drop into publish-ready posts. Jasper has become a marketing platform whose "Jasper Agents" orchestrate multi-channel campaigns on a trained brand voice. Predis.ai converts a prompt or product catalog into visuals, video ads, and carousels. The cost gap is the whole argument: industry write-ups peg a full-service platform at roughly $4–6k/year versus $60–180k/year for an agency, and put execution at 70–80% of what agencies actually bill.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the deliverable is automatable, the markup on it evaporates. Agencies whose pitch is "we make the posts / write the content / build the report" are being repriced against software clients can run themselves. The honest nuance: these tools still need a human for taste, strategy, and brand judgment — but the volume work that padded retainers is going first.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Ocoya Jasper Predis.ai + Canva / Adobe Firefly (creative)

MOST EXPOSED: content, social, and creative-production agencies

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Run the tools yourself; climb the value ladder.
  1. Adopt these exact platforms internally to multiply output and slash cost — then compete on volume + speed + results at a price clients won't bother to beat, and keep the efficiency gain instead of passing it on.
  2. Sell what the tool can't: brand voice, taste, strategy, and a specific niche where judgment actually moves results.
  3. Reprice on outcomes or a productized retainer, never per-asset or per-hour (the per-asset model is exactly what the software undercuts).
REALITY CHECKCommodity content production as a standalone service is on its way out. The defensible business is "AI-leveraged operator with taste, in a niche" — not "we make the posts."
07
ROCKY.AI · WYSA · + OPENAI ACADEMY

$10–20/month AI coaches commoditize courses and generic coaching.

THE SHIFT → For coaches and course-sellers, the core product is packaged knowledge and "here's what to do" — exactly what these apps deliver 24/7 for the price of a coffee.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · active, category-specific VELOCITY · STEADY LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

Steady consumer interest in AI coaching/companion apps, but no breakout spike this pass. The broader "free & cheap AI for everything" wave — and today specifically the ChatGPT superapp pushing one app that both advises and acts — quietly normalizes "why pay a human for the plan?" The threat is a slow, grinding repricing of "information," not a headline today.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Named, live products undercut the model directly: Rocky.ai runs daily accountability, goal-setting, and executive role-play with free and low-cost tiers; Wysa offers an AI life-coach chat from $9.99/month with 150+ exercises; AI fitness coaches charge ~$20/month for unlimited access against $40–100 per session for a human trainer. Above them sits free knowledge — OpenAI Academy plus a chatbot that builds a personalized plan on request — and viral "ChatGPT runs your content like a $10k/mo agency, free" prompt packs.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the marginal cost of the information you sell drops to zero, you can't price the information. Durable value shifts to what these apps can't replicate — accountability you're held to by a real person, personalized judgment, lived credibility, community, and presence — and away from "I'll teach you the framework." Coaches and educators who sell information rather than transformation are the most exposed of all.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Rocky.ai Wysa AI fitness coaches OpenAI Academy (free) ChatGPT custom plans

MOST EXPOSED: coaches · course creators · info-product sellers · framework-led consultants

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Sell transformation and accountability — not information.
  1. Rebuild the offer around what a $15/mo app can't give: real accountability (a person who holds you to it), community and belonging, live access, and personalized judgment. The static course is the part being commoditized — not you.
  2. Use AI as your between-sessions layer (daily check-ins, prompts) inside your program. Give clients the app experience plus you — don't pretend the app doesn't exist.
  3. Package a repeatable transformation system with measured outcomes. Build it like a product, deliver it like a high-touch relationship, measure it like a performance program. Short paid challenges (5–21 days) and paid communities convert and retain especially well.
REALITY CHECKThe pure info-product / course model is structurally challenged. If you sell knowledge, you have to move to selling change — a genuine reinvention of the offer, not a price change.

What survives is what no named model on this board can commoditize: judgment, accountability, trust, taste, relationships, and outcomes you put your name on.

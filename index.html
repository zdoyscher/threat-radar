01
CHATGPT · GPT-5.5 / 5.5 INSTANT · OPENAI

The chatbot on every client's desktop does the advisory work.

THE SHIFT → For ~$20/month, ChatGPT produces the strategy, plans, copy, and analysis clients used to pay an advisor to think through.

CRITICAL TRAJECTORY · worsening fast VELOCITY · SPIKING LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

The GPT-5.6 rumor window is now open. Multiple trackers put the strongest leak window in the first half of June — and as of this morning we're inside it, with prediction markets actively pricing a near-term drop (OpenAI has not confirmed it). At the same time OpenAI's new "dreaming" memory system — which auto-updates what ChatGPT remembers about your projects and constraints over time — is rolling out. Anticipation + a smarter-memory ChatGPT pulls every "ChatGPT replaced my ___" video back up the charts. Keeps the #1 spot pinned and spiking.

CONTEXT — WHY IT'S #1

This isn't a niche tool — it's the assistant clients already have open, now defaulting to GPT-5.5 Instant (which OpenAI says cut hallucinated claims ~52% vs the prior default on high-stakes prompts). ChatGPT runs around 880M–1B monthly users handling 2 billion+ queries a day and is now a top-5 website worldwide. An owner can paste their situation in and get a competent campaign plan, competitor breakdown, coaching reframe, or first-draft strategy in seconds — and memory now carries context across sessions. It doesn't have to beat you — it just has to be good enough that the client questions the gap between your invoice and a subscription.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

Fee compression at the root, hitting all three segments at once. One agency owner described a client demanding an 80% fee cut because "ChatGPT gets better results," then walking when they couldn't match it. Fresh 2026 industry surveys say roughly 1 in 4 agencies have already had clients ask for lower prices because AI makes the work look cheaper, and execution-layer tasks (content, scheduling, email, reporting, keyword research) are the first to get insourced. Money is visibly moving from "pay a human to advise" to "pay for AI."

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
ChatGPT (GPT-5.5 / 5.5 Instant) Claude (Opus 4.8) Google Gemini (3.5 Flash) Microsoft Copilot

MOST EXPOSED: ALL — agencies · consultants · coaches

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Sell the outcome, not the answer.
  1. In every sales conversation, say the line out loud: an AI gives you an answer; I deliver the result, the implementation, and the accountability for it. Make the client describe what happens after the answer — that gap is your offer.
  2. Stop shipping the strategy doc as the deliverable. Repackage and reprice around done-with-you / done-for-you execution and an outcome a prompt can't produce.
  3. Use GPT-5.5 / Claude yourself to cut delivery cost and lift margin — but never resell the raw output.
REALITY CHECKThis is a repositioning of what you sell, not a messaging tweak. If your core offer is "I'll tell you what to do," that offer is the thing under threat — plan to rebuild it around implementation, accountability, or a specific result.
02
CHATGPT AGENT · GEMINI SPARK · MANUS

Agents that operate your apps start doing the whole job.

THE SHIFT → Not just answering — these systems take multi-step actions across apps, files, inboxes, and the web, doing execution work end-to-end with a human only approving.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · emerging — climbing fast VELOCITY · SPIKING LIVE
WHY IT CLIMBED THIS PASS (VELOCITY · ▲ +1 → #2)

The agentic wave is the loudest story on the board this morning — and it just got a fresh accelerant. Manus made its Chat Mode free & unlimited for everyone, with a one-click upgrade straight into its autonomous Agent Mode — a viral moment across X and the AI subreddits this week. Layer on Gemini Spark (Google's 24/7 personal agent wired into Gmail, Docs & Slides) still rolling through AI Ultra beta, plus ChatGPT Agent, and "agents went mainstream" is trending hard enough to edge past Google to #2. On structural severity alone it still sits below the two giants — agents are emerging, not finished — so this is a velocity climb, not a severity upgrade.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

OpenAI folded Operator + Deep Research into ChatGPT Agent for web tasks like research, forms, and cross-site workflows (and reports its agent model now matches or beats the average human on OSWorld software-navigation tasks). Gemini Spark executes Workspace tasks on your behalf in the background, even after you lock the phone. Claude computer use (Opus 4.8) runs strong local file and document work. Manus runs long, multi-step autonomous jobs in its own sandbox — and is still independent: China blocked Meta's reported $2B takeover in April 2026. Alongside them, build-it-yourself tools like Lovable, v0, and Bolt let non-developers ship working sites and apps from a prompt.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

This is the frontier that, as it matures, automates whole processes rather than single tasks — the operational and build work many agencies and consultants resell. The honest reason it isn't #1 on severity: today's agents still complete only a fraction of open-ended desktop tasks reliably and mostly run human-in-the-loop. Loud attention, real trajectory — not yet a full replacement. But Manus going free pushes "try an agent on your actual work" to a much wider audience overnight.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Manus (Chat Mode now free) Gemini Spark (beta) ChatGPT Agent (Operator) Claude Computer Use Lovable / v0 / Bolt (build)

MOST EXPOSED: ops/automation & web-dev agencies · process-heavy consultants

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Get in front of the wave — don't wait to be hit by it.
  1. Start using ChatGPT Agent / Gemini Spark / Claude Computer Use / Manus on your own operations now — Manus is free to try this week, so there's no cost excuse. Build the cost-and-speed advantage before your clients or competitors do.
  2. Productize agent-run workflows ("we run X end-to-end") so the automation is your leverage and margin, not your replacement.
  3. Put it on a weekly watch. Its trajectory is the steepest on this board — re-check capability each pass (Gemini Spark went from announce to public beta in under three weeks; Manus went free overnight).
REALITY CHECKThis isn't a fire to put out today — agents still run mostly human-in-the-loop and miss a chunk of open-ended tasks. The real risk is complacency: early adopters become the low-cost, high-speed option that undercuts everyone who waited.

GOOGLE AI MODE + AI OVERVIEWS · GEMINI 3.5 FLASH

Search answers the question — so the click never reaches you.

THE SHIFT → Google now resolves the query on the results page. The blog-and-SEO funnel that quietly fed advisory businesses their leads is collapsing at the top.

CRITICAL TRAJECTORY · structural, irreversible VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT MOVED (THIS PASS · ▼ –1 → #3)

Nothing got smaller here — this is a pure velocity reshuffle. The Google I/O 2026 rollout (Gemini 3.5 Flash now the default in AI Mode, the new Daily Brief, the Gemini Omni video model) is still landing, but there was no acute spike today, so the agentic wave at #2 edged past it on momentum. Structurally it remains one of the two biggest threats on the board — a CRITICAL that a quiet news day must never bury.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

AI Mode (now default on Gemini 3.5 Flash) has passed 1 billion monthly users with queries doubling every quarter, and AI Overviews reaches the bulk of Google's search base. Around 64% of US Google searches now end without a click to the open web; when an AI Overview shows, ~83% of queries end with no click, and inside AI Mode it's about 93%. "How-to" and informational queries — exactly what advisors publish to get found — are hit hardest.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

If your pipeline depends on ranking and capturing the click, that pipeline is shrinking fast. Fresh 2026 data shows roughly 73% of B2B sites lost meaningful traffic across 2024–25 (average YoY decline ~34%), and big publishers like HubSpot and Chegg have reported steep organic-traffic losses amid AI search and algorithm shifts. Your content increasingly feeds the AI's answer without ever sending the reader to you — but note the flip side: brands cited inside AI answers are seeing more clicks, not fewer. Citation is the new ranking.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Google AI Mode + AI Overviews Perplexity ChatGPT Search

MOST EXPOSED: SEO/content agencies · content-led consultants & coaches · publishers

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Become the cited source — and stop renting traffic.
  1. Run the visibility audit (10 min): search your category in Google AI Mode, ChatGPT, and Perplexity. Note where you're absent. This is also your fix-list for Threat 05.
  2. Restructure cornerstone content for AEO/GEO: lead with the direct answer, clear headings, specific numbers, and schema. AI cites concise, authoritative, well-structured sources — not 2,000-word warm-ups.
  3. Build channels AI can't intercept: an owned email list, referral/partner loops, a community, and presence where humans still go (YouTube, LinkedIn, podcasts).
REALITY CHECKYou cannot out-SEO the zero-click era. The structural move is reducing dependence on organic clicks and rebuilding pipeline across owned + relationship channels. That's real work over months, not a plugin.

META ADVANTAGE+ / ANDROMEDA · + OPENAI ADS · GOOGLE PERFORMANCE MAX

Autonomous ad systems run the media buying you bill to manage.

THE SHIFT → The ad platforms now automate audiences, placements, budgets, and creative selection — the work paid-media agencies charge a retainer to do.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · accelerating VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT CLIMBED THIS PASS (▲ +1 → #4)

Ad automation is the loudest story in agency feeds right now. The Andromeda 2026 update ("creative is the new audience") is dominating paid-social write-ups this week, and OpenAI's self-serve Ads Manager — CPC bidding added May 5, now piloting beyond the US into Canada/Australia/NZ — has the "do we even need an agency to run this?" question trending among SMBs. Enough momentum to edge past Perplexity to #4 on the blend; structurally it's unchanged.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Meta Andromeda — a neural retrieval engine on NVIDIA Grace Hopper hardware — scans millions of ads in real time and shortlists the best before the auction even begins; Advantage+ hands audiences, placements, and budgets to the algorithm, and advertisers turning on its AI targeting have seen around 22% higher ROAS than manual setups. On the other side, Google Performance Max + AI bidding decide placements, bids, and formats automatically, and OpenAI's new Ads Manager opens a third self-serve channel (CPC + CPM, no minimum spend) right inside ChatGPT. Agentic ad tools now let an assistant draft and launch a campaign from a single prompt, cutting setup from ~45 minutes of clicks to one instruction.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the platform optimizes better than a human dashboard-jockey, "we manage your ads" stops justifying a retainer. The defensible work narrows to creative, offer, measurement, and strategy — a much smaller surface than the full-service media-buying fee most agencies still quote.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Meta Advantage+ / Andromeda Google Performance Max OpenAI Ads Manager

MOST EXPOSED: paid-media / performance agencies and freelancers

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Move up the stack the algorithm can't reach.
  1. Stop selling "campaign management" as the core deliverable. Reposition and reprice around the three layers automation can't do: creative volume & testing, offer/funnel strategy, and first-party-data + measurement (connecting CRM/site data is shown to cut CAC meaningfully — and it's provable value).
  2. Become the creative engine. Advantage+ rewards many creative variants; sell creative testing at scale as your product.
  3. Tie the fee to growth/outcomes, not hours spent in a dashboard.
REALITY CHECKThe flat "% of ad spend to run the account" model is structurally shrinking as the platforms automate the buying. This needs an offer migration to creative + strategy + data — not a louder pitch for the old service.

PERPLEXITY · + COMET BROWSER

The answer engine is the buyer's new gatekeeper.

THE SHIFT → Prospects research — and shortlist — vendors inside an AI. If it doesn't name you, you're not in the running, however good you are.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · rising — slipped to #5 VELOCITY · RISING LIVE
WHY IT MOVED (THIS PASS · ▼ –1 → #5)

Still genuinely rising — Comet is live on every platform, free worldwide, and Perplexity keeps pushing into agentic buying — but it had no fresh breakout today, so the ad-automation spike (Andromeda 2026 + OpenAI Ads expansion) edged past it. The structural threat is unchanged; this is a velocity reshuffle, not a downgrade.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Perplexity (~$22B valuation, 100M+ monthly users, used inside 92% of the Fortune 500) is the purest example: its Comet browser — free worldwide since late 2025 — will compare options across retailers and add the pick to your basket. The buying behavior is mainstream: recent 2026 buyer surveys find roughly 51% of B2B software buyers now begin research in an AI chatbot, and 85% of buyers stick to a "day-one" vendor list shaped before they ever talk to you. Being un-cited by the AI is the new page-10-of-Google.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

The moment of competitive selection moves out of your control. A growing share of prospects arrive pre-filtered by a model that already decided who's "worth contacting." You can be excellent and never get the call — because the shortlist was written by an AI that never heard of you.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Perplexity + Comet ChatGPT (B2B research default) Google Gemini

MOST EXPOSED: any advisory firm that wins clients through inbound & discovery

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Engineer the consensus that makes the AI recommend you.
  1. Manufacture consensus signals. AI recommends what it sees agreed across independent sources. Get listed and reviewed on your niche's directories and review sites (G2-type), earn third-party mentions, and show up genuinely in the Reddit threads, YouTube videos, and forums where your buyers ask questions.
  2. Publish citable proof: named case studies with real numbers. Vague "we're great" copy is invisible to a model; a specific, verifiable result is quotable.
  3. Niche down hard. AI surfaces the specialist ("best X for Y"), not the generalist. Tighten positioning to a category you can credibly own.
REALITY CHECKThis compounds slowly — citations build over weeks to months and can't be bought overnight. Start now; once you're the consensus pick, it's a moat competitors can't quickly copy.

OCOYA · JASPER · PREDIS.AI

All-in-one content agents produce the work you bill for.

THE SHIFT → Dedicated platforms now generate, design, schedule, and report on content end-to-end — the execution layer that fills agency timesheets.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · steady erosion VELOCITY · STEADY LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

A mature, crowded category — consistent buyer interest but no acute spike in view velocity this pass. Real structural pressure, low headline volatility. Watch item: Jasper Agents orchestrating multi-channel campaigns blurs this card into the agents cluster at #2 — if that framing goes viral, this one heats up.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

These tools market straight at you: Ocoya's pitch is literally "You don't need to hire a social media agency anymore," auto-turning a product listing or price drop into publish-ready posts. Jasper has become a marketing platform whose "Jasper Agents" orchestrate multi-channel campaigns on a trained brand voice. Predis.ai converts a prompt or product catalog into visuals, video ads, and carousels. The cost gap is the whole argument: industry write-ups peg a full-service platform at roughly $4–6k/year versus $60–180k/year for an agency, and put execution at 70–80% of what agencies actually bill.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the deliverable is automatable, the markup on it evaporates. Agencies whose pitch is "we make the posts / write the content / build the report" are being repriced against software clients can run themselves. The honest nuance: these tools still need a human for taste, strategy, and brand judgment — but the volume work that padded retainers is going first.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Ocoya Jasper Predis.ai + Canva / Adobe Firefly (creative)

MOST EXPOSED: content, social, and creative-production agencies

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Run the tools yourself; climb the value ladder.
  1. Adopt these exact platforms internally to multiply output and slash cost — then compete on volume + speed + results at a price clients won't bother to beat, and keep the efficiency gain instead of passing it on.
  2. Sell what the tool can't: brand voice, taste, strategy, and a specific niche where judgment actually moves results.
  3. Reprice on outcomes or a productized retainer, never per-asset or per-hour (the per-asset model is exactly what the software undercuts).
REALITY CHECKCommodity content production as a standalone service is on its way out. The defensible business is "AI-leveraged operator with taste, in a niche" — not "we make the posts."

ROCKY.AI · WYSA · + OPENAI ACADEMY

$10–20/month AI coaches commoditize courses and generic coaching.

THE SHIFT → For coaches and course-sellers, the core product is packaged knowledge and "here's what to do" — exactly what these apps deliver 24/7 for the price of a coffee.

HIGH TRAJECTORY · active, category-specific VELOCITY · STEADY LIVE
WHY IT'S MOVING (THIS PASS)

Steady consumer interest in AI coaching/companion apps, but no breakout spike this pass. The broader "free & cheap AI for everything" wave — Manus going free, ChatGPT's ad-supported free tier — quietly normalizes "why pay a human for the plan?" The threat is a slow, grinding repricing of "information," not a headline today.

CONTEXT — CURRENT STATE

Named, live products undercut the model directly: Rocky.ai runs daily accountability and goal-setting frameworks for under $15/month; Wysa offers an AI life-coach chat from $9.99/month; AI fitness coaches like Ray charge ~$20/month for unlimited access against $40–100 per session for a human trainer. Above them sits free knowledge — OpenAI Academy plus a chatbot that builds a personalized plan on request — and viral "ChatGPT runs your content like a $10k/mo agency, free" prompt packs.

IMPACT — WHAT IT MEANS FOR THE MONEY

When the marginal cost of the information you sell drops to zero, you can't price the information. Durable value shifts to what these apps can't replicate — accountability you're held to by a real person, personalized judgment, lived credibility, community, and presence — and away from "I'll teach you the framework." Coaches and educators who sell information rather than transformation are the most exposed of all.

FIELD — THE COMPETITORS DRIVING THIS
Rocky.ai Wysa Ray (fitness) OpenAI Academy (free) ChatGPT custom plans

MOST EXPOSED: coaches · course creators · info-product sellers · framework-led consultants

▶ PLAN OF ACTION
Sell transformation and accountability — not information.
  1. Rebuild the offer around what a $15/mo app can't give: real accountability (a person who holds you to it), community and belonging, live access, and personalized judgment. The static course is the part being commoditized — not you.
  2. Use AI as your between-sessions layer (daily check-ins, prompts) inside your program. Give clients the app experience plus you — don't pretend the app doesn't exist.
  3. Package a repeatable transformation system with measured outcomes. Build it like a product, deliver it like a high-touch relationship, measure it like a performance program. Short paid challenges (5–21 days) and paid communities convert and retain especially well.
REALITY CHECKThe pure info-product / course model is structurally challenged. If you sell knowledge, you have to move to selling change — a genuine reinvention of the offer, not a price change.
One pattern across all seven — and the thread running through every plan above: these systems aren't replacing the skill of agencies, consultants, and coaches — they're dissolving the scarcity that let them charge for it. What survives is what no named model on this board can commoditize: judgment, accountability, trust, taste, relationships, and outcomes you put your name on. Note the honest split in the reality checks: the smaller threats have clean quick fixes, but the biggest ones (01, 06, 07) demand a genuine repositioning of what you sell — and no amount of easy reading makes that optional.
